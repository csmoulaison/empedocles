We wanted to test what increasing xmm register utilization 
would do, allowing us to do less reads from memory.

xmm12-xmm15 were populated with v4_sign_mask, v4_zero, 
v4_one, and v4_inf at the render_region label. These were 
selected on the basis of common usage throughout the loop.

The results were very surprising. Pre-packing the data into
the registers caused it to be slower than doing memory 
reads. I have no compelling ideas as to why. Maybe using the
registers in this way caused issues with utilization of 
specific units in the CPU that could be fixed with some kind
of interleaving.

The verdict: We are sticking with direct memory reads,
miraculously.
